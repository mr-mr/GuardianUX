At the end of a week in which the prime minister has apologised to Liverpool fans for terrible wrongs that took place 23 years ago at Hillsborough, the atmosphere on Wearside promises to be poignant and evocative. "It will be highly charged because of what happened on Wednesday," said the Sunderland manager, Martin O'Neill. "It will be a very emotional occasion with Liverpool fans coming in their droves."

While Brendan Rodgers's players aim to begin correcting a disappointing start to the campaign, Sunderland have surmounted problems with their new pitch's drainage and finally play their first home league game of the season.
